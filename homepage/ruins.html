THE CIRCULAR RUINS

No one saw him disembark in the unanimous night, no one saw the bamboo canoe sink into the sacred mud...

He knew that this temple was the place required for his invincible intent.

He knew that his immediate obligation was to dream.

He wanted to dream a man; he wanted to dream him in minute entirety and impose him on reality.

This magic project had exhausted the entire expanse of his mind.

At first, his dreams were chaotic; then they became dialectic in nature.

Within a year he had come to the skeleton and the eyelids.

He dreamed an entire man — a young man who could not open his eyes.

Fire would magically animate the dreamed phantom.

With relief, with humiliation, with terror, he understood that he also was an illusion, that someone else was dreaming him.
